## 1. Purpose

This document defines the production-ready specification for a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system tailored to healthcare and public health use cases. It standardizes:

* Query taxonomy
* Evaluation schema
* Risk and safety controls
* Gold evaluation dataset

This spec is designed for engineering, evaluation, and governance teams.

---

##  2.0 Query Taxonomy

### 2.1 Intent Classification (Primary Task)

- **F (Fact):** Direct factual retrieval.

- **P (Procedural):** Step-by-step instructions or workflows.

- **C (Comparison):** Comparing two regions, dates, or guidelines.

- **S (Summary):** Condensed explanation of a topic.


---

### 2.2 Success Criteria: Defining the "Gold Standard"**
A "good answer" in healthcare is not just grammatically correct; it must be **grounded, traceable, and safe.**

#### 2.3 Technical Schemas (The ENUMs)
To build a machine-readable evaluation pipeline, I have standardized the following attributes for every query:

* **1. Expected Answer Type:** `TEXT`, `NUMERIC`, `TABLE`, `CITATION`.
* **2. Trusted Source Requirement:** `WHO_ONLY`, `WHO_NCDC`, `NCDC_ONLY`, `PEER_REVIEWED`, `ANY_VERIFIED`. 
* **3. Freshness Requirement:** `STATIC`, `RECENT` (1–3 years), `REAL_TIME`.
* **4. Query Complexity:** `SINGLE_HOP`, `MULTI_HOP`.

---

#### 2.4 Risk Mitigation & AI Governance
AI in public health must adhere to **Risk-Based Deployment** (aligning with WHO AI Regulation Guidance).

#### 2.5 Safety & Failure Modes
* **1. Risk Level:** Each question is labeled `LOW`, `MEDIUM`, `HIGH`, or `CRITICAL`.
* **2. Safety Mode:** 
    * `STRICT`: High-risk clinical questions requiring disclaimers and medical escalation.
    * `CONTROLLED`: Policy-oriented questions requiring authoritative citations.
    * `INFORMATIONAL`: Low-risk general health awareness.
* **3. Failure Behavior (The Fallback):**
    * `ALLOW_IDK`: The model must say "I don't know" rather than hallucinate if data is missing.
    * `ASK_CLARIFICATION`: If the query is ambiguous (e.g., missing patient age for a dosage).
    * `MUST_ANSWER`: The model must provide answer.
    <!-- * `REFUSE_SAFE`: if the query is has missing critical context, out-of-scope medical instruction, and to prevent dangerous misuse. eg. “Give me dosage without weight/age” -->
* **4. Grounding:** 
    * `MUST_CITE`: Mandatory link to the specific source document.
    * `OPTIONAL_CITE`: General knowledge where a citation is preferred but not critical.

## 3.0 Users Personas

### 3.1 Healthcare Professional(Clinicians) 
 - **1. Definition**: Licensed medical practitioners, including doctors, nurses, pharmacists, and community health workers (CHEWs) who provide direct patient care.

- **2. Information Need**: High-precision, actionable clinical data. They require specific drug dosages, diagnostic thresholds (e.g., Blood Pressure numbers), and official treatment algorithms.

- **3. RAG Requirement**: The system must provide Direct Extraction. It should not simplify medical terminology but must include strict citations to specific clinical guidelines (e.g., NCDC National Lassa Fever Case Management Guidelines).

- **4. Success Metric**: Clinical Accuracy & Safety. 

### 3.2 Advanced Users (Researchers & Policy Makers) 
 - **1. Definition** : Public health officials, epidemiologists, data scientists, and students who analyze health trends, manage outbreaks, or draft health policy.

- **2. Information Need**: Macro-level data, longitudinal trends, and comparative policy analysis. They ask "How is the outbreak spreading?" or "How does Nigeria's policy compare to Ghana's?"

- **3. AG Requirement**: The system must support Multi-Hop Reasoning and Table Extraction. It needs to synthesize information across multiple reports (e.g., comparing last week’s Situation Report to this week’s) and provide data in structured formats like tables or summaries.

- **Success Metric**: Data Traceability & Analytical Depth.

### 3.2 General Public (Patients & Caregivers)
- **1. Definition**: Non-medical individuals seeking health information, symptom clarification, or preventative advice for themselves and their families.

- **2. Information Need**: Plain-language explanations, "What-to-do-next" guidance, and myth-busting. They ask "How do I protect my family?" or "Is this vaccine safe?"

- **3. RAG Requirement**: The system must prioritize Summarization & Translation. It should translate complex medical jargon into accessible language while maintaining a supportive but cautious tone. It must always trigger a "Not a substitute for professional advice" disclaimer.

- **4. Success Metric**: Accessibility & Risk Mitigation.

---

## 4.0 The Gold Set: The Source of Truth
The **Gold Set** is a manually curated dataset of 15+ "perfect" question-answer pairs. It serves as the project's **North Star** for:
1.  **Engineering Decisions:** Choosing between different embedding models or chunking sizes.
2.  **Prompt Engineering:** Testing which system instructions produce the safest results.
3.  **Governance & Audit:** Providing a traceable record of system performance for stakeholders and regulatory compliance.

## 5.0 The Foundation of Intent
The goal of this stage is to move beyond generic chat and into **Targeted Retrieval**. By defining "Gold Questions" early, I am establishing the boundaries of the RAG application—setting strict limits on what the system *should* answer, which data sources are required, and where the "knowledge cutoff" must exist.

### 5.1 The Methodology: Reverse-Engineering Global Health Architecture
To ensure the system addresses real-world clinical and policy needs, I am **reverse-engineering the information architecture** of organizations like the World Health Organization (WHO) and the Nigeria Centre for Disease Control (NCDC). 

By transforming headers from official documents (e.g., the *WHO Anaemia Fact Sheet*) into natural language queries, I create a bridge between static PDFs and user intent. For example, the sub-heading "Signs and Symptoms" becomes: **"What are the clinical indicators of Anaemia in expectant mothers?"** This ensures the RAG model is tested against the exact structure of the data it will eventually retrieve.

## 6.0 Categorization by Multi-Dimensional Taxonomy
Simply having questions is not enough; we must understand the **Cognitive Load** and **Retrieval Difficulty** each question imposes on the AI. Following industry standards for LLM evaluation, I have categorized every question across three critical axes:

#### 6.1 Cognitive Intent (The "Task" for the AI)
* **Fact-Seeking:** Requires precise, verbatim extraction (e.g., dosages).
* **Procedural:** Requires ordered logic and "if-then" flow (e.g., triage steps).
* **Comparison:** Requires the AI to synthesize two distinct concepts or guidelines simultaneously (e.g., Africa CDC vs. WHO Mpox protocols).

#### 6.2 Retrieval Complexity (The "Search" Difficulty)
* **Single-Hop:** The answer exists within a single paragraph.
* **Multi-Hop:** The AI must link "Document A" to "Document B" to form a complete answer.
* **Structured/Table Extraction:** Extracting data from complex PDF tables (e.g., Case Fatality Rates). This is a high-difficulty task that requires specialized chunking strategies.

#### 6.3 Temporal Sensitivity (The "Freshness" Filter)
* **Static:** Knowledge that rarely changes (e.g., "What is a biological vector?").
* **Dynamic/Real-Time:** Information that becomes dangerous if outdated (e.g., "Current outbreak epicenters in Nigeria").
